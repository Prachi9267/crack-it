Internship Opportunities

1.) Deshaw Desis Ascend

Timeline: August-September [off campus]

About: D.E. Shaw is a global investment and technology development firm founded in 1998. The Ascend Educare Program is a six-month mentorship initiative for women in tech, launched in 2021. Through this program, students gain a better understanding of work culture and real-world projects at D.E. Shaw. Additionally, D.E. Shaw provides a stipend of approximately INR 50,000 for the entire duration of the program.

Eligibility Criteria

  • The Ascend Educare Program is only for women in engineering and is not open to male students.
  • Second- and third-year female students of B.Tech/B.E. programs are eligible.
  • Third- and fourth-year students in a five-year dual degree program are eligible.
  • First-year students in a two-year M.Tech/MS/MCA program are eligible.
  • Second-year students in a three-year MCA program are also eligible.

Procedure

Round 1: Resume Shortlisting

This is an elimination round, where your resume plays a crucial role.If your resume is shortlisted, you advance to Round 2. This step also involves completing a detailed form that asks for personal details, college information, relevant transcripts, and responses to essay questions.

Tips: Do not write fake things in order to get the opportunity, highlight your skills and projects done till then.

Round 2: HackerRank Online Test

This test was taken on HackerRank with proctoring. It comprised of three sections: Aptitude, Computer Science Fundamentals(time complexity related ques, DBMS and OOPS), and Coding(around three questions of medium to hard level difficulty). The aptitude questions included topics from probability, profit, and loss, covering most of the syllabus of quantitative aptitude. The coding section consisted of three coding questions which were of medium to hard difficulty level.One of the questions was very tough, and students who completed even half of this question were invited for further rounds.

Tips: For coding questions, practicing on platforms like Leetcode can help build a basic understanding and a problem-tackling approach.

Round 3: Technical and HR Interview

This round lasts about 45 minutes. The interviewer can ask for an introduction, followed by coding questions of medium to hard difficulty.Topics included linked lists and graphs.In the HR interview, questions testing soft skills will be asked.

Tips for HR/Personal Interview: Questions that can be asked include "Introduce Yourself," "How do you plan to manage the workload of this program alongside your college studies?", and "What qualities or attributes set you apart as the ideal candidate for this program?".

2.) Google STEP

Timeline: November - December [off+on campus]

About: The Google STEP (Student Training in Engineering Program) internship is a 12-week summer program designed for first and second-year undergraduate students in technical fields, particularly computer science. It provides hands-on experience with real Google projects, mentorship from experienced engineers, and opportunities to develop both technical and professional skills.

Procedure

  • A robust Resume: A good resume is crucial to get you through the initial stage of the internship. It should contain all important details and be clean and concise.

    • Tips: You can use Overleaf for resume building.

  • Telephonic follow-up: Some of the questions can include your preferred language, and questions related to the projects mentioned in your resume.
  • Final Interview: It takes place in two rounds. A helpful tip for the interviews is to communicate clearly and mention the approaches that you are following to solve the question.

Tips: Practice mock interviews (technical + behavioral).Study topics like arrays, linked lists, trees, and graphs, etc.Communication is just as important as coding skills during interviews. You can also use Overleaf for resume building.

3.) Uber Star

Timeline: January [on campus]

About: She++ is a professional development summit hosted by Uber to empower women and people from underserved communities in Denmark, Brazil, India and the Netherlands to find and achieve their career ambitions.

Eligibility

  • Must be in Computer Science, Engineering, or related Software Engineering fields.
  • Must be a 2nd and 3rd year student who identifies as female, a person with disabilities, or LGBTQIA+.
  • Must be attending an accredited engineering college or university.

Procedure

  • Application Screening: After submitting the application, candidates undergo an initial screening based on their academic performance, relevant experience, and overall profile.
  • Online Assessment: Shortlisted students take a coding test of 1 hour and 30 minutes to solve four questions related to coding general CS fundamentals and DSA.
  • Hiring Manager Interview: Those selected for the Uber She++ program can appear directly for a hiring manager interview for the Uber STAR internship program.
  • There is no stipend for the She++ program. She++ participants get an opportunity to appear directly for a hiring manager interview for the Uber STAR internship program. If selected, they can get a stipend of up to INR 1,00,000 per month.

Tips: Build some good projects showcasing the skills you mentioned in your resume. Practice coding regularly.In an interview, stick to what you know.

4.) LinkedIn Coachin

Timeline: January-February [off+on campus]

About: CoachIn is a mentorship program by LinkedIn. All women engineering students across India in the B.Tech course (2nd-year college students) with at least 70% CGPA were eligible for the program.

Procedure

  • Registration: It requires basic information like name, college name, specialization, graduation year, and LinkedIn profile.
  • Hackerrank Test: This test will have around three coding questions of easy to medium level.
  • Phone Screening Round: The final round is a phone call asking basic questions like why you want to join the program, what keeps you moving forward, and your experience with open source.

Tips: The LinkedIn profile was a major factor, so you need to polish your LinkedIn profile before applying. Good DSA skills are needed to clear the coding round.In the Phone Screening Round, be honest, clear, and concise in what you are speaking.

6.) JPMC SEP

Timeline: March [for 3rd year internship]

About: This program is for 3rd year internship students.Like most of internships, it begins with submitting your resume.

Procedure

  • First Round: An online assessment with around two coding problems to be done in 45 minutes. Example questions that were asked include a dynamic programming (DP) problem and a greedy algorithm problem.
  • Round 2: A behavioral interview focused on assessing soft skills, problem-solving mindset, and cultural fit.Questions were unique for each candidate and included scenarios like "Give a recent example where you learned and gained experience" and "Describe a situation where you felt you had not communicated well".
  • Round 3: An invitation to participate in the Code for Good Hackathon held at JPMC’s office.The hackathon required solving a real-world problem statement.

6.) Microsoft Explorer Internship Program

About: Explore Microsoft is an 8-week summer internship program specifically designed for first- and/or second-year college students.The program allows the opportunity to experience the product development cycle's primary phases: Design, Build and Quality.

Procedure

  • It starts with selection through your resume.
  • The initial interview is one 30-minute phone call.These vary in structure, but mostly behavioral questions are asked.However, it is a best practice to also practice coding just in case.
  • The final round consists of 2 (45-minute) rounds with 1 interviewer each.Each interview is expected to give one programming question each.

Tips: Most people haven’t done their Data Structures and Algorithms course by initial interview stage, so there are a limited number of topics you need to cover: Strings, Arrays, Dictionaries/HashMaps, Linked Lists, and Binary Trees.For questions, solve LeetCode.
