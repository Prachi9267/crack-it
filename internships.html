Deshaw Desis Ascend

About

D.E. Shaw is a global investment and technology development firm founded in 1998.Through this program, students gain a better understanding of work culture and real-world projects at D.E. Shaw. Additionally, D.E. Shaw provides a stipend of approximately INR 50,000 for the entire duration of the program.

Timeline

August - September

Procedure

Eligibility Criteria

    The Ascend Educare Program is only for women in engineering and is not open to male students. The eligibility requirements are as follows:

  • second- and third-year female students of B.Tech/B.E. programs
  • Third- and fourth-year students in a five-year dual degree program
  • First-year students in a two-year M.Tech/MS/MCA program
  • Second-year students in a three-year MCA program

Program Rounds

  • Round 1: Resume Shortlisting
    This is an elimination round, where your resume plays a crucial role. If your resume is shortlisted, you advance to Round 2.
    Tips: Do not write fake things in order to get the opportunity; highlight your skills and projects done till then.
  • Round 2: HackerRank Online Test
    This test is taken on HackerRank with proctoring and comprises three sections. Section 1 is Aptitude, with medium-hard difficulty questions from probability, profit and loss, and quantitative aptitude. Section 2 is Computer Science Fundamentals, with questions on time complexity, DBMS, OOPs, and some bug-finding questions. Section 3 is Coding, with three medium-to-hard difficulty questions. One question was very tough, and students who completed even half of it were invited for further rounds.
    Tips: For coding questions, practicing on platforms like Leetcode can help build basic understanding and the problem-tackling approach.
  • Round 3: Technical and HR Interview
    Only a few reach the interview stage, and this round lasts about 45 minutes. The interviewer may ask for an introduction, followed by medium-to-hard coding questions on topics like linked lists and graphs. The HR interview includes questions testing soft skills.
    Tips: Questions that can be asked in the HR or personal interview include "Introduce Yourself," "How do you plan to manage the workload of this program alongside your college studies?" and questions about handling difficult project situations or explaining technical concepts.
Google STEP

About

The Google STEP (Student Training in Engineering Program) is a 12-week summer program for first- and second-year undergraduate students in technical fields, particularly computer science. The program provides hands-on experience with real Google projects, mentorship, and opportunities to develop both technical and professional skills.

Procedure

A robust Resume

A good resume is crucial to get through the initial stage and should include important details like contact information, GitHub URL, and LinkedIn profile URL.

Telephonic follow-up

Some of the favorable questions include preferred language and questions related to the projects mentioned in your resume.

Final Interview

It takes place in two rounds, and a helpful tip is to communicate clearly and mention the approaches you're following to solve the question.
Tips: Practice mock interviews, study topics like arrays, linked lists, trees, and graphs, and remember that communication is just as important as coding skills during interviews.

Uber Star

About

She++ is a professional development summit hosted by Uber to empower women and people from underserved communities in Denmark, Brazil, India, and the Netherlands to find and achieve their career ambitions, fostering a community for the next generation in tech.

Eligibility

  • Computer Science, Engineering, or related Software Engineering fields.
  • Second- and third-year students who identify as female, people with disabilities, or LGBTQIA+.
  • Must be attending an accredited engineering college or university.

Procedure

  1. Application Screening: Candidates undergo an initial screening based on their academic performance, relevant experience, and overall profile.
  2. Online Assessment: Shortlisted students take a coding test with four questions of varying difficulty, which are related to coding, general CS fundamentals, and DSA.
  3. Hiring Manager Interview: Those selected for the Uber She++ program can appear directly for a hiring manager interview for the Uber STAR internship program.

Note: There is no stipend for the She++ program. She++ participants get an opportunity to appear directly for a hiring manager interview for the Uber STAR internship program. If selected, they can get a stipend of up to INR 1,00,000 per month.

Tips

  • Build good projects to showcase the skills mentioned in your resume.
  • Practice coding regularly.
  • In the interview, stick to what you know.
LinkedIn CoachIn

About

CoachIn is a mentorship program by LinkedIn. It is open to all women engineering students across India who are in their second year of a B.Tech course with at least a 70% CGPA.

Procedure

  1. Registration: This requires basic information like name, college name, specialization, graduation year, and LinkedIn profile.
  2. Hackerrank Test: The test will have around three coding questions of easy to medium level.
  3. Phone Screening Round: The final round is a phone call asking basic questions like why you want to join the program, what keeps you moving forward, and your experience with open source.

Tips

  • Your LinkedIn profile is a major factor, so you need to polish it before applying and get it reviewed.
  • Good DSA skills are needed to clear the coding round.
  • For the Phone Screening Round, be honest, clear, and concise in what you are speaking.
  • Actively participate in all sessions, follow the learning path, and take mock tests and interviews seriously.
  • Be interactive with your peers and mentor; your mentor is there to help and offer advice.
